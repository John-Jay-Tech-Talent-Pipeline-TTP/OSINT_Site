OSINT

OSINT stands for Open Source Intelligence
What is OSINT?

OSINT is the data, and analytics you find on sources that is easily available and open to use for everyone.

Most Common Examples of Where You Can Conduct OSINT:
  		•  Instagram
  		• Facebook
  		• Google
  		• And much more!

    Remember: OSINT is not necessarily open surface data. OSINT is not just limited to search tools. When you do a “deep web” of mass data tools, they can also be counted as means of osint.


    Pros
  • Not illegal since OSINT pertains to info that is already out in the public
  • Various sources of info out there
  • Costs less to gather information compared to using tools specifically designed to collect info

    Google Dorking

    What is Google Dorking?

    It is basically a way hackers use to get through a site to find if they have any security holes in the computer codes in order to get the sites valuable and non-public information.


    Examples where Google Dorking is used:
  		• Ethical Hacking (Kali Linux, Kali Nethunter)
  		• Data Collection Phase of OSINT

      Pros
    • Efficient way of utilizing Google Search Engine
    • Can recover an abundance of information that may not be that visible on a website
      Cons
    • Can be illegal if used for malicious purposes (e.g. cyberattack reconnaissance)
    • Can collect sensitive info from results
    • Could be used to exploit security vulnerabilities of a website
